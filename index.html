Cybersecurity Culture Assessment

Measure your organization’s security culture across knowledge, comprehension, behaviour, and attitude. Select a persona and complete the survey to generate an instant dashboard highlighting strengths and the highest-priority improvement areas.

Dimension 1

Knowledge

Assesses baseline awareness of cyber risks, policies, and controls (e.g., phishing signs, MFA use, data handling).

Dimension 2

Comprehension

Tests whether people understand why controls matter, can interpret risk scenarios, and choose safe decisions.

Dimension 3

Behaviour

Evaluates what people do in practice: reporting incidents, using secure workflows, and avoiding risky shortcuts.

Dimension 4

Attitude

Measures motivation, ownership, and belief that cybersecurity is a shared responsibility across the organization.

How Culture is Measured and Improved

Culture maturity is scored on a 1–5 scale per question and normalized to a 0–100 score for each dimension. The dashboard classifies each area as: Needs Focus (<60), Developing (60–79), or Strong (80+).

  • Measure: Collect persona-specific responses to ensure relevance for general users, technical staff, and leadership.
  • Interpret: Compare dimension scores to identify root gaps (e.g., strong knowledge but weak behaviour).
  • Improve: Apply targeted interventions like practical simulations, manager-led coaching, and policy simplification.
  • Reassess: Repeat monthly or quarterly to track trend lines and verify that interventions improved outcomes.

Tip: A healthy culture balances all four dimensions; sustained behaviour and attitude gains typically produce the most resilient outcomes.
